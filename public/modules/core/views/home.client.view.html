How does it work?

1. Create a research project.

Your research project should have a title and a description outlining the aim of your research.

2. Assign project tasks.

The project tasks are instructions to your research participants on how they can gather data for you.

3. Send out project.

Let your participants download the insighting app, then scan the QR code generated by the project.

4. Recieve insights!

The insights gathered by your participants will show up here on the webapp.

Research project

The research project is at the base of this webapp.
Each project starts with a title and a description, aimed to help you and your research participants understand the aim of the project.

To create a new project, fill in the form and hit create.

“Bob has been asked to find out how people in his community views the public garden space. He creates a research project called ‘What does the garden mean?’ and adds a short description, outlining the purpose and benefit of the project.”

Project tasks

The project tasks are what you are asking your participants to do.
Each task has a title, usually in the form of an instruction and a short description.
The tasks can be of different types, each type relates to a set of data that can be obtained from a smartphone.

“Bob wants to understand how the community view the public garden, so in his research project he creates a set of tasks for his research participants. The first task he creates is a photo task, asking the participant to take a photo of something they like in the garden space.
The second task is a text task, asking the participant to write a short note every time they visit the garden.”

Mobile app

The mobile app is the part of insighting that your research participants are going to see. The app runs on a smartphone and asks the user to login/signup and then scan the QR supplied by the researcher. When the QR code is scanned, the app shows the participants the assigned projects tasks and lets them complete the tasks using their phone.

“Bob is still researching the community’s view on the garden space and has created a set of tasks for his participants. Now he goes to the park to find people who can partake in the research, he finds a number of people and asks them to download the app and scan the code.”

Insights

When a research participant completes a task, the data submitted is sent back to this webapp and will appear in the research project.
These snippets of data are what we call insights, they will contain different kinds of data depending on the task and most of them will have extra data, such as gps position and data/time stamp, generated by the phone app.

Now it is up to you to use these insights and do something fantastic with them!

“Bob is now at home, watching the photos and text snippets appear on the webapp. He notices how several people have taken photographs of a certain part of the park and now has a deeper understanding of how the community views the garden space.”
